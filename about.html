About

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, devices, and data from digital attacks, theft, damage, or unauthorized access, using technology, processes, and people to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of digital assets, crucial in our tech-reliant world to prevent financial loss, data breaches, and operational disruption for individuals, businesses, and critical infrastructure. It involves defending against threats like malware, phishing, and ransomware through tools (antivirus, firewalls), policies (strong passwords, MFA), and awareness training.

Key Aspects

  • Protection : Safeguarding against cyber threats like hacking, phishing, ransomware, and malware.
  • Assets Protected: Includes hardware, software, data, networks, and connected devices (IoT).
  • Core Goals (CIA Triad): Ensuring Confidentiality (preventing unauthorized viewing), Integrity (preventing unauthorized alteration), and Availability (ensuring access when needed) of data and systems.

Why It's Important

  • Digital Reliance: Most of life, from work to social, is online, making systems vulnerable.
  • Cost of Breaches: Cybercrime costs billions and causes significant financial and reputational damage.
  • Critical Infrastructure: Essential services like power grids and pipelines are managed online and at risk.
