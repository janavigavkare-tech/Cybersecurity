Safety Tips

To stay cyber safe, use strong, unique passwords and a password manager, enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) everywhere, think before clicking suspicious links/attachments (phishing), keep all software updated, use VPNs on public Wi-Fi, back up data regularly, and be cautious sharing personal info online. Securing devices with PINs/biometrics and installing antivirus software are also key steps to prevent unauthorized access and malware.

Password & Account Security

  • Strong, Unique Passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols; don't reuse passwords across sites.
  • Password Manager: Use one to generate and store complex passwords securely. Phishing & Social Engineering
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA/2FA): Enable it on all accounts for an extra layer of security (e.g., code sent to phone).

Phishing & Social Engineering

  • Think Before You Click: Be wary of unsolicited links or attachments in emails, texts, or social media.
  • Verify Requests: Be suspicious of requests for personal info, even if they seem to be from trusted sources.
  • Limit Sharing: Don't overshare personal details on social media, and be cautious with friend requests from strangers.
